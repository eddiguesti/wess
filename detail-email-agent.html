Tour Operator
Email Agent

An AI system that monitors your hotel inbox around the clock. It reads tour operator emails, classifies intent, pulls the correct data, and drafts responses. Your team reviews and sends, or the system sends autonomously once trust is established.

How It Works

Step by Step

Every email that arrives is processed through a structured pipeline. The agent classifies, retrieves, and drafts with full traceability.

Intent Classification

The AI reads the incoming email and classifies it: booking request, rate query, contract update, availability check, special offer, or general enquiry. Each category triggers a different processing path.

Data Retrieval

Based on intent, the agent pulls the relevant data: rates from Opera, availability from the channel manager, contract terms from the rate sheet database. Every figure comes from a verified source.

Response Drafting

The agent composes a response using the correct data, matching the tone and format your team already uses. Rate sheets, contracts, and confirmations are generated and attached automatically.

Human-in-the-Loop

Every draft starts in a review queue. Over time, routine responses (availability confirmations, standard rate replies) can be set to auto-send. Complex negotiations always stay in the queue.

What It Handles

Every TO Communication, Covered

Rate Requests:Tour operator asks for current rates for a specific period. The agent pulls the correct seasonal rate from Opera and responds with the formatted rate sheet.
Booking Confirmations:Reservation request comes in. The agent checks availability, confirms the booking in Opera, and sends the confirmation back to the TO with all relevant details.
Contract Negotiations:When a TO proposes rate amendments or new terms, the agent flags it for human review with full context: current contract terms, proposed changes, historical rates with that operator.
Special Offer Distribution:When you create a new promotion or early bird offer, the agent sends it to your entire TO list, personalised per operator, with the correct rates for their market segment.
Availability Queries:"Do you have 3 Superior rooms 15-22 December?" The agent checks Opera in real time and responds within minutes, not hours.
Version Tracking & Audit Trail

Who Has What Rates

Knowing which tour operator has which version of your rates is one of the biggest operational headaches. The email agent solves it.

Rate Version Registry

Every rate sheet sent is logged: which version, when, who opened it. You always know which operator has which prices.

Outdated Rate Alerts

When you update rates, the system flags every tour operator still working off the old version. It can automatically send update notifications, or queue them for your team to review first.

Why this matters: Right now, if you update your rates in November for the upcoming high season, there's no easy way to know which of your 50+ tour operators received the new rates and which are still quoting the old ones. The agent tracks every version, every send, every acknowledgement.

The "Ping Pong" Problem, Solved

A tour operator books a room at a rate that doesn't match Opera. Today, this gets discovered at check-in or during month-end reconciliation. The email agent cross-checks every incoming booking against the contracted rate and flags discrepancies immediately. Resolution happens in email, not at the front desk.

Catches wrong room category pricing (e.g., TO charged Standard rate for a Superior room)
Detects outdated seasonal rates being applied (e.g., low season price used during high season dates)
Flags supplement discrepancies (meal plans, transfers, special requests billed incorrectly)
How It Learns

Gets Smarter Over Time

Starts with Templates

We load your existing email templates, rate sheet formats, and standard responses. From day one, the agent matches your team's style and language.

Learns from Edits

Every time your team edits a draft before sending, the agent learns from the correction. If Sarah adds a specific line for German tour operators, or Peter prefers a different closing for long-standing partners, the agent picks up these patterns.

Continuous Improvement

Over time, the queue shrinks. Routine responses that pass review without edits get promoted to auto-send. Complex cases stay in the queue. Faster without being reckless.

What We Need from the Process Map

To Build This Right

Process Map Required

To build the email agent accurately, we need to understand the exact email flow. This comes from your team during the process mapping phase.

Which mailboxes?:How many inboxes handle TO communications? Is it per property, per person, or centralised?
Who handles what?:Which team members handle which types of email? Are there specialists for specific markets or operators?
What systems provide the data?:Where do current rates live? Opera? Excel? A combination? Which is the source of truth?
What's the approval flow?:Which responses need manager approval? Which can the team send directly? What are the escalation rules?
What templates exist?:Current email templates, rate sheet formats, contract templates. These become the agent's starting knowledge.
