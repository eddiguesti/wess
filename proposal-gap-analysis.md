## GAPS — Not yet addressed or underweight

### 1. Centralised Head Office Operations (Call point #5)
**Problem:** Peter is restructuring into central reservations, sales, marketing, HR, finance. He needs a system that supports this.
**Current state in proposal:** Mentioned briefly in the dashboard section but not as its own concept.
**What to do:** This is bigger than a dashboard — it's about how ALL the tools we're proposing serve the centralisation goal. Could be a framing slide early on that ties everything together: "Every module we're proposing feeds into one centralised operation."

### 2. Cloud Strategy / Marriott Constraints (Call point #6)
**Problem:** Marriott requires on-premise servers. Cloud transition is planned 2025–2027. Peter needs hybrid/transitional architecture.
**Current state in proposal:** Not addressed at all.
**What to do:** Add a section or card about our approach to infrastructure — how we build cloud-native but can work alongside Marriott's on-prem requirements. This matters because it shows we understand the constraints.

### 3. Reliability + Support Model (Call point #7)
**Problem:** This is a DECISION DRIVER. Peter will pick worse tech with better support. "Friday breakdown, Monday answer is not acceptable."
**Current state in proposal:** Mentioned briefly in the purchasing section (European hosting, 24/7 support) but not as a cross-cutting commitment.
**What to do:** This needs its own section or a prominent commitment — our SLA, support hours, response times, hosting approach. This is a differentiator and a buying criterion. Should be near the end, before the CTA.

### 4. Budget / Investment Framing (Call point #11)
**Problem:** Peter's budget is €100k–€200k+. He's not price-sensitive if value is clear.
**Current state in proposal:** No pricing or investment framing at all.
**What to do:** Don't need exact prices yet (process map comes first), but should frame the investment level. Something like "The full programme is a significant investment — but it pays for itself in staff hours, error reduction, and operational speed. We'll scope precise costs after the discovery."

### 5. "What He Wants Us To Be" — Strategic Partner Positioning (Call point #12)
**Problem:** Peter doesn't want a vendor. He wants a systems integrator, automation layer builder, strategic partner, and practical problem solver.
**Current state in proposal:** Implied but never stated directly.
**What to do:** Add a slide near the intro or CTA that positions Jengu explicitly as a long-term partner, not a project vendor. "We're not here to deliver software and leave. We're building the operating layer for your business — and we'll be here as it evolves."

### 6. "Central Nervous System" Framing (Call insight — The Opportunity)
**Problem:** The strongest positioning is: Opera = core data, our system = orchestration layer, email = input stream, outputs = bookings/contracts/finance/reporting.
**Current state in proposal:** Not framed this way.
**What to do:** This could be a powerful diagram or visual in the proposal — showing Opera at the centre, our layer wrapping around it, with email flowing in and all outputs flowing out. This is the "big picture" slide Peter needs to see.

## WHAT STILL NEEDS EXPLAINING

### To Peter / WESS:
1. **How the email agent actually connects** — do we need access to their mail server? Is it a forwarding rule? An API integration? They'll want to know the mechanics, not just the concept.
2. **What "process map" actually delivers** — a document? A diagram? A roadmap with timelines? Be specific about the deliverable.
3. **How we handle the Marriott constraint** — can our tools sit alongside Marriott's required infrastructure without conflict?
4. **Our support model** — response times, hosting location, escalation path, what happens at 2am on a Saturday.
5. **How phasing works commercially** — is each phase a separate contract? A retainer? A fixed project fee?

### From Peter / WESS (we need to learn):
1. Which Sage version they run
2. How night audit currently works
3. Their chart of accounts structure
4. Which tour operators have API capabilities vs email-only
5. Current rate structure (seasonal, contract, promotional)
6. Who the key people are in each department
7. What their Marriott tech obligations actually are
8. Current Material Control setup and workflows
