## PREVIOUSLY IDENTIFIED GAPS — Now addressed

### 1. Centralized Head Office Operations (Call point #5)
**Problem:** Peter is restructuring into central reservations, sales, marketing, HR, finance. He needs a system that supports this.
**Now addressed:** 'Centralization Is the Direction' in the intro frames the goal. 'One View. The Whole Group.' and 'Centralized Head Office. One View.' in the dashboard section tie all modules back to the centralization objective.

### 2. Cloud Strategy / Marriott Constraints (Call point #6)
**Problem:** Marriott requires on-premise servers. Cloud transition is planned 2025–2027. Peter needs hybrid/transitional architecture.
**Now addressed:** 'Marriott Is a Constraint' in the intro acknowledges it upfront. 'Working Within Your Constraints' section covers 'Sits Alongside Marriott' and 'Cloud-Native, Hybrid-Ready', confirming our systems work regardless of where the data sits.

### 3. Reliability + Support Model (Call point #7)
**Problem:** This is a DECISION DRIVER. Peter will pick worse tech with better support. 'If something breaks Friday... maybe I get an answer on Monday.'
**Now addressed:** 'Reliability Over Cleverness' in the intro sets the tone. 'Friday Problems Get Friday Answers' is its own section covering European Hosting, Responsive Support, and Proactive Monitoring. Includes Peter's quote about the Friday/Monday problem.

### 4. Budget / Investment Framing (Call point #11)
**Problem:** Peter's budget is €100k–€200k+. He's not price-sensitive if value is clear.
**Now addressed:** 'What This Costs' section includes line-item pricing for every module (from €105,500 total setup, €4,350/month). Notes that final scope and cost depend on the discovery. Nothing is committed until scoped together.

### 5. Strategic Partner Positioning (Call point #12)
**Problem:** Peter doesn't want a vendor. He wants a systems integrator, automation layer builder, strategic partner, and practical problem solver.
**Now addressed:** 'A Partner, Not a Vendor' section states it directly. Four pillars: 'We integrate your systems.', 'We build the automation layer.', 'We help shape what comes next.', 'We work in the real world.'

### 6. Email as the Nervous System (Call insight — The Opportunity)
**Problem:** The strongest positioning is: Opera = core data, our system = orchestration layer, email = input stream, outputs = bookings/contracts/finance/reporting.
**Now addressed:** 'Email Is the Nervous System' in the intro frames it clearly. The email agent detail pages show how email flows through classification, data retrieval, and response drafting, with Opera as the source of truth throughout.

3. **How phasing works commercially** — is each phase a separate contract? A retainer? A fixed project fee?
