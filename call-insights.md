# WESS — What We Learned from the Call with Peter Edler
> **Source of truth for the proposal. Always refer back to this.**

---

## Core Objective (Peter's "Dream Outcome")

A fully integrated, centralised, largely automated operating system for the hotel group that:
- Eliminates manual work and duplication
- Connects currently disconnected systems
- Works within Marriott constraints
- Handles the reality of a tour-operator-dominated market (email + Excel)
- Is future-proof (cloud, scalable, modern)

> "Everything under one umbrella... one system... joined up."

---

## 1. System Integration (Top Priority)

**What he has now:**
- Oracle Opera PMS (main "source of truth")
- Oracle Symphony
- Sage
- Email + Excel + manual processes
- No communication between systems

**What he wants:**
- Opera → Sage → Booking → Contracts → Finance — all aligned
- No re-entry of data
- No mismatches between systems

> "I have the rate in Opera... but not in Sage... booking comes by mail... then re-entered manually."

---

## 2. Eliminate Manual "Ping Pong" Processes

**Current reality:**
- Contracts sent by email
- Rates manually uploaded
- Bookings printed + re-entered
- 9–10 people involved
- Constant errors

**What he wants:**
- End-to-end automation of commercial workflows
- Contract distribution → tour operators
- Offer updates
- Booking intake
- Price validation
- Invoice consistency

> "Print it... put it in... failure quote step one... wrong price... wrong room... wrong date."

---

## 3. Email → Structured Data Automation (Huge Opportunity)

**This is where Peter got visibly excited about our system.**

**Current:**
- ~90% of communication via email
- Emails = contracts, bookings, offers

**What he wants:**
- An intelligent email layer that reads incoming emails, extracts bookings/rates/contracts, and pushes into systems automatically

> "What I saw on your email system... another planet."

**This is the major entry point.**

---

## 4. Channel Manager + Booking Engine (Reimagined)

**Current:**
- No real channel integration
- Tour operators don't sync
- Rates distributed manually

**What he wants:**
- A hybrid distribution system
- Central booking engine
- Push contracts to operators
- Handle direct bookings
- Integrate with Marriott
- One place to manage all rates & offers

> "My dream... I can have it in my booking engine... send contracts to all tour operators."

---

## 5. Centralised Head Office Operations

**He is restructuring into:**
- Central reservations
- Central sales
- Central marketing
- Central HR
- Central finance

**What he needs:**
- A system that supports centralisation
- Shared data layer
- Cross-department workflows
- Visibility across all properties

---

## 6. Cloud Strategy (With Constraints)

**Problem:**
- Marriott currently requires on-premise servers
- Cloud transition planned (2025–2027)

**What he wants:**
- A hybrid / transitional architecture
- Possibly hosted externally "in between"
- Future-ready for cloud migration
- Flexibility + compliance

---

## 7. Infrastructure + Reliability Matters (Critical)

**He cares about:**
- Maintenance
- Uptime
- Local support
- Failover

> "If something breaks Friday... answer Monday is not acceptable."

**He will choose slightly worse tech BUT better support + reliability.**

---

## 8. Procurement & Inventory Systems Gap

**Current:**
- Material Control being phased out
- No replacement ready

**What he wants:**
- Centralised purchasing + inventory system
- Reliable, integrated, real-time

---

## 9. Process Mapping FIRST (Critical Expectation)

**He strongly agrees with this approach.**

**What he expects:**
- Not just software — but diagnosis + design
- Map real workflows
- Identify bottlenecks
- Then propose solution

> "You have to show me what your system is capable... business is different here."

---

## 10. Must Work in a Pragmatic Environment

**Reality:**
- Majority tour operators
- Email-driven workflows
- Limited system interoperability
- Some modern pockets (HR via social media, etc.)

**What he needs:**
- Pragmatic innovation
- NOT "replace everything"
- Bridge old + new

---

## 11. Budget & Buying Criteria

**Budget:** €100k–€200k+ is acceptable

**Decision drivers:**
1. Does it improve operations?
2. Is it reliable?
3. Is there strong maintenance/support?
4. Does it integrate everything?

**Not price-sensitive if value is clear.**

---

## 12. What He Really Wants Us To Be

Not just a vendor.

1. **Systems Integrator** — Connect everything into one ecosystem
2. **Automation Layer Builder** — Especially around email + workflows
3. **Strategic Partner** — Help design the future operating model
4. **Practical Problem Solver** — Must work in messy, real-world conditions

---

## The Opportunity (Our Positioning)

Peter wants us to build a **"central nervous system"** for the hotel group where:
- Opera = core data
- Our system = orchestration layer
- Email = input stream
- Outputs = bookings, contracts, finance, reporting

---

## 5 Things We MUST Emphasize in the Proposal

1. End-to-end integration (Opera → Sage → Email → Booking)
2. Email automation as a core engine
3. Centralised operations platform
4. Phased implementation (works now, evolves later)
5. Reliability + support model
