Dynamic
Pricing

Smart, automated rate adjustments for OTA and direct bookings based on occupancy, demand signals, and competitor rates. Not for tour operator contracts. Positioned as forward-looking infrastructure that becomes more valuable as direct booking share grows.

Where It Applies

Not Everything. The Right Things.

Important context: Most WESS business comes from tour operator contracts with fixed seasonal rates. Dynamic pricing does not apply to those. Those rates are agreed annually and do not change with daily demand. Dynamic pricing applies to OTA and direct booking channels, where rates can flex in real time.

Dynamic Pricing Applies

Booking.com, Expedia, direct website bookings, walk-in rates, last-minute availability. These channels benefit from real-time rate adjustments based on demand.

Fixed Seasonal Rates

Tour operator contracts, group bookings, allotment blocks. These stay at the agreed seasonal rates. The system manages them centrally but doesn't dynamically adjust them.

Today, the OTA/direct share may be relatively small. But it's growing. And as it grows, the revenue impact of dynamic pricing compounds.
Even with a small OTA share, dynamic pricing can capture significant incremental revenue during high-demand periods when the hotel would have sold the room anyway, just at a lower price.

Occupancy-Based Adjustments

As a property fills up, rates increase automatically. At 50% occupancy, the rate might be at the base level. At 80%, it rises. At 95%, it's at a premium. The curve is configurable. You control how aggressive the pricing gets.

Competitor Rate Monitoring

The engine monitors competitor hotel rates on OTAs in real time. If competitor properties are selling out and raising rates, your rates can adjust accordingly. If the market is soft, the engine can hold or lower rates to maintain competitiveness.

Demand Signal Analysis

Beyond occupancy and competitors, the engine factors in search volume, flight booking trends to Mauritius, local events, public holidays in source markets, and historical patterns.

Rate Guardrails

You set the floor and ceiling. The engine never prices below your minimum or above your maximum. Weekend rates, holiday rates, and blackout dates are all configurable. The engine executes the strategy. It does not replace it.

Seasonal Contract Management

TO Contracts, Managed Centrally

One Place for All Rate Tiers

While dynamic pricing handles the OTA/direct channels, the same system manages the fixed seasonal rates for tour operator contracts. Low season, mid season, high season, all defined centrally, assigned per tour operator, and distributed through the email agent to each tour operator. One system, two pricing models, zero confusion.

Season definitions Define exactly which dates fall into low, mid, and high season. The system applies the correct rate automatically based on stay dates.
Contract-specific rates Each TO can have their own contracted rates per season. The system tracks which operator has which rate and ensures the correct price is quoted every time.
Year-on-year comparison Compare contracted rates across years. See at a glance whether you're giving TUI a better or worse rate than last year, and by how much.
Future Value

Infrastructure That Grows with You

The strategic position: Dynamic pricing is forward-looking infrastructure. Today, most WESS revenue comes from TO contracts with fixed rates. But the direct channel is growing, and every point shifted from TO to direct means higher margins and more pricing flexibility. The engine is ready when the market catches up.

Year 1: Dynamic pricing on OTA/direct channels. Even a small share benefits from demand-based pricing. Foundation is laid.
Year 2-3: As the website and direct booking engine drive more direct traffic, the dynamic engine has more inventory to optimise. Revenue impact grows proportionally.
Long term: Revenue intelligence layer on top: demand forecasting, market segment analysis, pickup pace tracking. The data accumulated by the pricing engine becomes an asset for strategic pricing decisions across all channels, including TO negotiations.
