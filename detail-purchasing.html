The Urgent Gap

Why This Can't Wait

The situation: Oracle Material Control is being phased out. Sustaining support only: bug fixes, no new features, no replacement timeline. The alternative under consideration is hosted on a server in Thailand with no 24/7 maintenance. A Friday breakdown means a Monday answer. For a hotel group that operates every day, that is unacceptable.

Material Control is end-of-life:Oracle has moved it to sustaining support. No new features, no roadmap, no guaranteed long-term future. Staying on it is a ticking clock.
No Oracle replacement:Oracle has not shipped a successor product. Hotels still on Material Control are left to find their own solutions.
Thailand server risk:The alternative under consideration has infrastructure with no SLA. Downtime during a weekend or holiday could mean days without purchasing capability across the group.
Centralisation blocked:Peter is building a centralised head office. Without reliable purchasing software, centralised procurement oversight is impossible.

Core Features

Everything You Need, Nothing You Don't

Purchase Orders

Create, approve, and send purchase orders from any device. Each PO links to a supplier, a department, and a budget. The head office sees every order across every property in real time.

Supplier Communications

Orders go to suppliers electronically, email or portal. Supplier confirmations, delivery notes, and invoices are tracked against the original PO. Three-way matching (PO, delivery note, invoice) runs automatically.

Stock Levels & Alerts

Real-time stock tracking across all outlets. Automatic reorder alerts when stock falls below configurable thresholds. Par level management for F&B, housekeeping, maintenance, and front office supplies.

Approval Workflows

Configurable approval chains. A kitchen order under EUR 500 might auto-approve, while a maintenance order over EUR 2,000 needs the GM's signature. Every approval is logged with timestamp and approver.

Goods Receiving

When a delivery arrives, staff log it against the PO. Quantity discrepancies, damaged goods, and substitutions are recorded immediately. The system flags any delivery that doesn't match the order.

Multi-Outlet Management

Each WESS property has multiple outlets: restaurants, bars, spa, pool bar, rooms division. Each outlet manages its own orders and stock, but the head office sees everything consolidated. Inter-outlet transfers are tracked.

European Hosting & Support

24/7 Support. Your Timezone.

Directly Addressing the Thailand Concern

Hosted on European cloud infrastructure with guaranteed uptime SLAs. Support available 24/7, not just during Bangkok office hours. A 4pm Friday breakdown gets resolved on Friday. This is the biggest difference from the alternative under consideration.

Cloud-Native Architecture

No on-premise server. No hardware to replace. No backups to manage. Runs in the cloud, scales automatically, maintained by the hosting provider. Your team focuses on purchasing, not IT.

Data Residency

Data hosted in European data centres, compliant with EU data protection regulations. Automated backups with point-in-time recovery. Disaster recovery built in.

The support difference: With the Thailand-based alternative, a critical issue on Friday evening means purchasing is offline until Monday. With European-hosted infrastructure and 24/7 support, the same issue is triaged within the hour and resolved within the SLA. For a hotel group running 7 days a week, this is not optional. It is essential.

What We Need from the Process Map

To Match Your Existing Workflows

Process Map Required

The purchasing system needs to mirror how WESS actually procures goods today. We need to understand the current workflow before we can build the replacement.

Approval chains:Who can approve what? What are the thresholds? Does it vary by property, by department, by supplier type?
Supplier relationships:How many suppliers? Are there preferred supplier agreements? Contracted pricing? How do suppliers currently receive orders?
Outlet structure:What are all the outlets across all properties? How are they organised? Which ones share suppliers? Which have unique procurement needs?
Current Material Control workflows:What does the team use Material Control for today? What works well? What's broken? This ensures we don't lose functionality in the transition.
Budget structure:How are purchasing budgets allocated? By property? By department? By quarter? The approval logic depends on this.
