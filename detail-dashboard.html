Central Operations
Dashboard

One unified view of the entire WESS group. Live occupancy, revenue by channel, rate parity status, outstanding communications. All in one place. The product that makes a centralized head office possible.

Peter's Vision

Centralized Head Office. One View.

The goal: Peter is building a centralized head office. The objective is to maintain one unified view of the business. Not five different logins. Not waiting for property managers to email reports. Not piecing together fragmented data. One screen, every number that matters, updated in real time.

From Fragmentation to Clarity

Today, understanding the business means checking Opera for occupancy, Sage for financials, email for TO communications, and spreadsheets for the rest. The dashboard pulls all of it into one live view.

What It Shows

Everything That Matters

Live Occupancy

Real-time occupancy across all WESS properties. Today's occupancy, this week's forecast, next month's projection. Colour-coded by performance against target. At a glance, you know which properties need attention.

Bookings by Channel

How many bookings came through Booking.com vs Expedia vs direct vs tour operators this week? This month? This quarter? See channel mix shifting in real time and spot trends early.

Rate Parity Status

Are your rates the same across all channels right now? The dashboard shows parity status at a glance. Green for matched, red for violations. Click through to see which channel is off and by how much.

TO Communications

Outstanding tour operator emails awaiting response. How many are in the queue? How long has the oldest been waiting? Which need a human review? The email agent's status at a glance.

Revenue by Outlet

How is each restaurant, bar, spa, and rooms division performing? Daily revenue by outlet, compared to budget and previous year. F&B performance at a glance across the entire portfolio.

Platform Options

Notion or Standalone

Option A: Built into Notion

Peter already utilizes Notion. We will build the dashboard and morning briefing directly within his existing workspace using Notion databases, automated pages, and API integrations. Zero new tools to learn. Data flows into where he already works.

Option B: Standalone Web App

Purpose-built dashboard accessible from any browser. Live charts, interactive maps, drill-down analytics. Shareable with the wider team without Notion access. Can still push the morning briefing to Notion.

Our recommendation: Start with Notion integration for the morning briefing (fast to deliver, already in use) and build the standalone dashboard in parallel. Both coexist: Notion for Peter's daily workflow, standalone for the operations team and GMs.

What We Need from the Process Map

To Build the Right Dashboard

Process Map Required

A dashboard is only as good as the data it shows. We need to understand what Peter and the team must see to run the enterprise.

What KPIs matter? Occupancy, ADR, RevPAR, GOP. Which metrics does Peter look at first? What does the board report require? What do the GMs need?
What data sources exist? Opera, Sage, Symphony POS. Which systems hold the data? How is it currently accessed?
What decisions are made based on this data? Understanding what actions follow from the numbers helps us prioritize what's on the dashboard vs. what's in a drill-down report.
Who else needs access? Is this Peter-only? Does each GM need a property-specific view? Does the finance team need their own slice? The access model determines the architecture.
Peter's current Notion setup What does the existing Notion morning briefing look like? What does he like about it? What's missing? We build on what works.
